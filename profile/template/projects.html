EECS 418/Fiat Chrysler Class Project

About

This project was a requirement for EECS 418 Power Electronics, with Prof. Heath Hoffman.

The objective of this class project was to design and build a DC to DC converter that mimics a lower scale voltage stabilizer module used in vehicle stop/start applications. The purpose of the DC to DC converter (voltage stabilizer module) is to prevent customer functions from reacting negatively when the engine restarts and causes a severe load.

Characteristics of Boost Converter design

Minimum input voltage 6V
Boosted output voltage level 12V
Output power @ 12 V 24W
Switching Frequency 200 kHz or higher
Peak Inductor Current ripple:
 10%
Peak Output voltage ripple:
 5%
Efficiency >85%
Reverse battery protection Yes
Voltage control loop Yes
Current control loop Optional
Mode switching circuit Optional

Our design ended up getting 97% efficiency. Input was measured at 5.89 V and 2.58 A, output was 12 V and 1.314 A. After the class ended, I redesigned the PCB to make it smaller and achieve the output current closer to 2 A.

Feel free to contact me for more details and a full report on the project!

Solid State Lab

About

This lab was a requirement for EECS 423 Solid State Device Laboratory with Professor Jerzy Kanicki. Goal of this project was to create working n-type transistors, of varying sizes, on a silicon wafer. Fabrication labs were held in the Lurie Nanofabrication Facility. Methods used included:

  • Photolithography
  • Etching
  • Deposition
  • Oxidation
  • CVD/PVD

After the wafer was fabricated, data was collected to correlate the experimental data with theoritical data. Capacitance based on doping concentration and oxide thickness, Voltage threshold based on gate length, and more were calculated

The following plot includes data taken from the fabricated wafer. The gate-source voltage vs the drain current of the transistors is plotted. The different traces are for the 5 different transistors sizes on the wafer. A slight shift is present as the gate length increases, meaning more voltage is required to "turn on" the transistor with increasing gate length.

Variable Frequency Electro-Acoustic Device

About

My senior design project was a year-long project while I was an undergraduate student at the University of North Texas. It was a major requirement for my Bachelors of Science in Computer Engineering. Our team researched solutions for Sensorineural hearing loss(SHL). We developed a hearing aid that was capable of compensating the inaudible range of any user. This could be achieved by having the user customize the settings of our device on first use.

Thermal Power Validation Group, Metrology Capability Study

About

This project was my main assignment during my summer internship with Intel. I worked on a variety of Intel products, setting up test environments and running specific workloads to gather power and thermal data. I worked with python scripts to automate the processes.

Goals

Identify principal sources of variation; quantify and minimize their contribution to overall variability of power and thermal measurements performed on a TPV system. Quantify and compare total system-to-system variability for thermal and power measurements across multiple product segments/platforms (client and server). Results and Details are under NDA
