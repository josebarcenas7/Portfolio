DC-DC Power Converter

About

This project was a requirement for EECS 418 Power Electronics (include link to class website), with Prof. Heath Hoffman.

This project was developed in collaboration with FCA (include link) motors, they supplied the budget for the parts and the PCB Board, they also reviewed our design and provided guidance. Silicon Wafer Processing

About

This project was a requirement for EECS 423 Solid State Device Laboratory with Professor Jerzy Kanicki Goal of this project was to create a working n-type transistors on a silicon wafer. Fabrication labs were held in the Lurie Nanofabrication Facility. Methods used included:

  • Photolithography
  • Etching
  • Deposition
  • Oxidation
  • CVD/PVD

Variable Frequency Electro-Acoustic Device

About

The project was a yearlong project for my senior design capstone course at the University of North Texas. It was a major requirement for my Bachelors of Science in Computer Engineering. Our team researched solutions for Sensorineural hearing loss(SHL). We developed a hearing aid that was capable of compensating the inaudible range of any user. This could be achieved by having the user customize the settings of our device on first use. Tech Specs Insert BOM (insert Slide Show)(Find way to imbed JPG on to site)

Thermal Power Validation Group, Metrology Capability Study

About

This project was my main assignment during my summer internship with Intel (include link). I worked on a variety of Intel products, setting up test environments and running specific workloads to gather power and thermal data. I worked with python scripts to automate the processes.

Goals

Identify principal sources of variation; quantify and minimize their contribution to overall variability of power and thermal measurements performed on a TPV system Quantify and compare total system-to-system variability for thermal and power measurements across multiple product segments/platforms (client and server) Results and Details are under NDA
